Choosing the Right HubSpot Alternative

HubSpot is an exceptional all-in-one platform, but it's not the right fit for every business. The most common reasons teams look beyond HubSpot come down to three factors: pricing that escalates sharply beyond the free tier, feature depth in specific areas like email automation or course delivery, and the desire for a more focused tool rather than a sprawling ecosystem. The good news is that the CRM and marketing automation space is mature enough to offer genuinely strong alternatives for every use case.

We've tested each of these alternatives hands-on and evaluated them against HubSpot across pricing, features, ease of use, and ideal customer profile. Here's our detailed breakdown to help you find the right match.

Best for Email Automation: ActiveCampaign

If your primary need is sophisticated email marketing and automation, ActiveCampaign is the strongest HubSpot alternative available. Its visual automation builder surpasses HubSpot's in both depth and flexibility — you can create complex branching workflows with conditional logic, predictive sending, split testing, and goal tracking that would require HubSpot's Professional tier ($890/month) to match. ActiveCampaign delivers this starting at just $29/month.

Where ActiveCampaign particularly excels is deliverability. Independent tests consistently show 93–96% inbox placement rates, compared to HubSpot's 89–93%. For businesses where email is the primary revenue channel — e-commerce, SaaS newsletters, course launches — this difference translates directly to ROI. The trade-off is that ActiveCampaign's CRM is more basic than HubSpot's, and there's no built-in CMS or service hub. But if email automation is your priority, it's the clear winner.

Best for Enterprise: Salesforce

For mid-size to large organizations with complex sales processes, Salesforce remains the industry standard CRM — and for good reason. Its customization depth is unmatched: custom objects, advanced workflow rules, territory management, CPQ (configure-price-quote), and an ecosystem of thousands of AppExchange integrations make it adaptable to virtually any business model. Where HubSpot starts to strain under enterprise complexity, Salesforce is purpose-built for it.

The trade-off is implementation complexity and cost. Salesforce requires more setup time, often benefits from a dedicated admin, and the per-user pricing adds up quickly across large teams. But for organizations that need granular control over their sales pipeline, deep reporting with custom dashboards, and the ability to model complex B2B relationships, Salesforce delivers capabilities that HubSpot's CRM simply can't match at scale. Starting at $25/user/month for Essentials, it's also more affordable than many expect at the entry level.

Best for Course Creators: Kajabi

Kajabi occupies a unique niche that HubSpot doesn't directly address: it's an all-in-one platform built specifically for knowledge entrepreneurs, course creators, and coaching businesses. Where HubSpot gives you CRM and marketing tools that you'd need to stitch together with a separate course platform (Teachable, Thinkific, etc.), Kajabi bundles course hosting, membership sites, landing pages, email marketing, and payment processing into a single platform.

The automation capabilities are simpler than HubSpot's, but they're tailored for the creator workflow — automated drip sequences tied to course progress, abandoned cart recovery for digital products, and pipeline tracking for coaching clients. At $69/month for the Basic plan (0% transaction fees), Kajabi eliminates the need for 3–4 separate subscriptions. For anyone selling digital products or online education, it's a more cohesive solution than cobbling together HubSpot with third-party course tools.

Best Budget Option: Brevo

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is the strongest option for businesses that need professional email marketing and basic CRM functionality without HubSpot's price tag. Its free tier includes 300 emails per day with unlimited contacts — a different model from HubSpot's contact-based pricing that can be more economical for businesses with large lists but moderate send volume. Paid plans start at just $25/month for 20,000 emails.

Brevo's automation builder covers the essentials well: welcome sequences, abandoned cart flows, lead scoring, and transactional emails are all included. The platform also offers SMS marketing, WhatsApp campaigns, and a basic CRM — making it a surprisingly complete toolkit for the price. Where it falls short compared to HubSpot is in reporting depth, native integrations (fewer than 100 vs. HubSpot's 1,500+), and the overall polish of the interface. But for startups, small businesses, and teams watching their software budget, Brevo delivers remarkable value.

Final Recommendations

The right HubSpot alternative depends entirely on what drove you to look for one. If you're frustrated by HubSpot's pricing for advanced automation, ActiveCampaign gives you more for less. If you've outgrown HubSpot's CRM capabilities, Salesforce is the natural step up. If you're a creator or educator, Kajabi replaces your entire tech stack. And if budget is the primary concern, Brevo delivers professional-grade email marketing at a fraction of the cost.

One important consideration: switching platforms involves migration effort — contact lists, automation workflows, integrations, and team training all need to be accounted for. We recommend running a focused 14-day trial of your top choice before committing, and mapping out your migration plan before canceling HubSpot. Most of these alternatives offer free trials or free tiers that make low-risk evaluation straightforward.
